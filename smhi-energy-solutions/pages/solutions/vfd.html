Variable Frequency Drives (VFDs)

Control motor speed based on demand — saving 20–60% on energy costs.

What VFDs Do

  • Control motor speed based on real-time demand, matching output to actual requirements
  • Ideal for HVAC systems, pumps, compressors, and chillers with variable loads
  • Eliminates wasteful full-speed operation common in traditional motor control systems

Electrical Savings

Typical Applications

  • HVAC fans and pumps: 30-50% savings
  • Industrial pumps and fans: 20-60% savings
  • Conveyor systems: 25-40% savings

Additional Benefits

  • Reduced mechanical stress on equipment
  • Extended equipment lifespan
  • Improved process control
  • Reduced maintenance costs

How VFDs Work

1

Convert Power

VFD converts incoming AC power to DC, then back to AC at the desired frequency

2

Adjust Frequency

By adjusting the frequency of the output power, motor speed is precisely controlled

3

Match Demand

Motor speed automatically adjusts to match real-time system demand
